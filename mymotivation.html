Theory

According to the Cambridge Dictionary, motivation is a noun used to designate the enthusiasm for doing something.

There are two types of motivation according to Ryan, R. M. and Deci, E. L. (Intrinsic and extrinsic motivation from a self-determination theory perspective: Definitions, theory, practices, and future directions, (2020)): intrinsic and extrinsic.

Intrinsic motivation corresponds to activities done because they provide joy and only because it fulfills us. E.g. Being motivated to eat candy because it will give us dopamine.
Often in contrast with intrinsic motivation, extrinsic motivation corresponds to behaviors done for an external reason. E.g. Being motivated to earn money with the end goal of buying the latest laptop to be the cool kid.

Related to university, the article Trajectories of motivation and their academic correlates over the first year of college, explain that during the first year of college, the intrinsic motivation (autonomous motivation) of students begin high but decreases during the year, while extrinsic motivation (controlled motivation) begins low and increase over the year.

To conclude this chapter, according to Usher, A. & Kober, N. (What is motivation and why does it matter? (2020)), we can note that motivation (intrinsic or extrinsic) is a really important factor in our day-to-day life, and even more so with education.
